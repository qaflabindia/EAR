# Policies

No policy is declared here. The Governor judges every policy attached to the
runtime or a workflow against the bare intent's context, before any step
runs -- the right place for a precondition genuinely knowable up front (a
loan amount, a credit score, as in the credit risk stack). This runtime's
intents carry only file paths; whether a cycle actually rested its
judgments on the workbook's own fields, never invented data, can only be
answered *after* the sandbox work runs -- so it belongs in the workflow's
own Deliverable and in the MIS Controller's validation, not in a Governor
policy asked to judge a question that hasn't happened yet. An earlier
"Data Provenance Control" policy tried exactly this and was removed after
it produced two contradictory verdicts (complies, then VIOLATED) against
the identical bare context across separate live runs -- an unanswerable
question judged as if it were answerable, blocking a cycle on pure sampling
noise rather than a real precondition.
